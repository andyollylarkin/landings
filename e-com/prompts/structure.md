## Voice & Tone Guidelines

- **Direct and clear:** No jargon, no fluffâ€”say what you mean

### Tone Adjustments by Section
- **Hero:** Confident, clear, problem-solving
- **Problem Section:** Empathetic, understanding, slightly frustrated (mirror their pain)
- **Solution Section:** Reassuring, capable, simplifying
- **Process Section:** Transparent, step-by-step, de-risking
- **Social Proof:** Credible, specific, results-driven
- **FAQ:** Patient, thorough, anticipating concerns
- **Final CTA:** Encouraging, low-pressure, inviting

## Key Messaging Frameworks Used

### Jobs-to-Be-Done (Throughout)
- Functional: "Get the data you need"

### Problem-Agitate-Solve (Section 3-4)

## Conversion Copywriting Techniques

### Specificity Over Generalities
- Bad: "Fast delivery"
- Good: "Data delivered in 48 hours"

### Benefits Over Features
- Bad: "We use rotating proxies"
- Good: "Get data from sites that block everyone else"

### Active Voice
- Bad: "Data is extracted by our team"
- Good: "Our team extracts your data"

### Concrete Examples
- Instead of: "We extract data"
- Use: "Extract 5,000 competitor prices daily"

### Social Proof Specificity
- Instead of: "Great service!"
- Use: "Prevented $200K in stockouts during Q4"
