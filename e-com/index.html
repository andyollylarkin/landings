Supply Chain Disruption Early Warning

The Client: A consumer electronics manufacturer.

The Challenge: Unexpected stock-outs of key components from suppliers were causing production delays, but the client had no visibility into their suppliers' downstream inventory levels.

The MRI Scan: We monitored the listings and inventory levels of their suppliers' finished goods on Amazon, Walmart, and Best Buy, tracking stock-out patterns and delivery estimate delays.

The Hidden Truth: The data revealed that when a specific supplier's products began showing "Temporarily Out of Stock" on Best Buy and shipping delays exceeding 2 weeks on Amazon, it was a leading indicator (by 10-14 days) of a component shortage that would impact the client's own factory.

The Result: The client used these early warnings to proactively secure alternative component suppliers, avoiding a 3-week production shutdown.

Averted an estimated $2M in lost production.
Gained a 10-day lead time on supply chain risk.

New Product Launch Intelligence

The Client: An emerging brand in the kitchen appliance space.

The Challenge: Preparing to launch a new smart coffee maker, they needed to optimize their launch strategy but lacked concrete data on competitor feature sets and introductory pricing.

The MRI Scan: We performed a deep analysis of all major competitor products on the big three platforms, scraping key features, review sentiment on specific functions, and historical pricing data for previous product launches.

The Hidden Truth: The analysis showed a gap in the market for machines with integrated grinders under $150. It also revealed that competitors consistently offered a 20% introductory discount during the first two weeks of launch, which was crucial for generating early review volume.

The Result: The client adjusted their feature set to emphasize the integrated grinder and adopted a successful competitive introductory pricing strategy, securing a #1 New Release badge on Amazon.

Achieved a #1 New Release rank in its category.
Sold out of its initial inventory within 10 days.

Dynamic Promotional Strategy

The Client: A large toy and game retailer.

The Challenge: Their Black Friday promotional planning was based on last year's data and guesswork, leaving money on the table and missing key competitive moves.

The MRI Scan: In the 4 weeks leading up to Black Friday, we tracked the promotional banners, "deal of the day" offers, and price changes for top-selling toy categories across competitor websites.

The Hidden Truth: We identified that a major competitor was running a "staged" promotion, discounting certain product categories each week to gauge price sensitivity, rather than waiting for the single Black Friday event.

The Result: The client mimicked this "staged" strategy, allowing them to optimize their final Black Friday discounts based on real-time data, maximizing margin while remaining competitive.

Increased Black Friday profit margins by 8% YoY.
Outperformed market sales growth by 15%.

Minimum Advertised Price Compliance Monitoring

The Client: A premium outdoor apparel brand.

The Challenge: Unauthorized resellers were consistently advertising their products below the Minimum Advertised Price (MAP), eroding brand value and angering their authorized retail partners.

The MRI Scan: We set up continuous, automated monitoring of their entire product catalog across all major e-commerce platforms, flagging any listing that violated the agreed-upon MAP policy.

The Hidden Truth: The data pinpointed that over 60% of MAP violations originated from just three specific unauthorized resellers who were using algorithmic repricing tools, and these violations spiked during key sales periods.

The Result: The brand was able to take targeted enforcement action against the specific violators and provide their authorized partners with proof of a level playing field.

Reduced MAP violations by 92% within 60 days.
Restored trust and revenue with key retail partners.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
GET TO KNOW US BETTER

What kind of data can MRI Data collect for me?

We can collect publicly available data from almost any online source, including:
- E-commerce & Retail: product details, pricing, reviews, and inventory levels from competitors
- Business Intelligence: company profiles, executive details, and firmographic data from directories like LinkedIn and Crunchbase
- Market Research: customer sentiments, trends, and brand mentions from social media platforms, forums, and news sites
- Real Estate: property listings, rental prices, and market trends
- Travel & Hospitality: hotel and flight prices, availability, and customer reviews

If you have a specific source in mind - just ask!

Are you limited to specific websites or countries?

No. Our data collection infrastructure is global and agnostic to source or language. We have the capability to gather data from websites worldwide, handling different languages, currencies, and site structures. Our technology is designed to see the structure beneath the surface, no matter where it is.

Is web scraping legal? How does MRI Data ensure it operates ethically?

Scraping publicly accessible data is legal, but it must be done responsibly. At MRI Data, compliance is core to our operations.
We ensure this by:
- Strictly adhering to a website's robots.txt file
- Respecting rate limits to avoid overloading servers
- Never attempting to breach authentication or access private data
- Complying with relevant data protection regulations like GDPR and CCPA
- Providing data for lawful purposes only - we believe in not just seeing the data, but seeing the ethical path to acquiring it!

Do you handle personally identifiable information (PII)?

We can, but with extreme caution and only under specific instructions. We act as a data processor and will work with you to ensure full compliance with privacy laws. Often, the key insights we uncover don't require raw PII, but rather aggregated, anonymized trends - the true "hidden truth" within the data.

In what format will I receive the data?

You will receive clean, structured, and analysis-ready data in your preferred format.
We routinely deliver data as:
- CSV/Excel files
- JSON or XML for API integration
- Direct feeds to your database or cloud storage (e.g., AWS S3, Google Cloud)

We transform the raw, unstructured data from the web into a format your team can immediately use.

How do you ensure data quality and accuracy?

We employ a multi-stage process:
- Parsing & Structuring: our advanced systems accurately extract and label data points
- Automated Cleaning: we deduplicate, standardize formats (e.g., dates, currencies), and handle missing values
- Quality Assurance: a sample of every dataset is manually reviewed by our data specialists to ensure it meets our high standards for accuracy and completeness

My target website is complex and uses a lot of JavaScript. Can you handle it?

Absolutely. Many modern websites rely on JavaScript to load content. Our "scanners" are equipped with headless browser technology that can fully render a webpage, interact with it if necessary, and extract data just as a human user would see it. We see past the dynamic surface to the static data beneath.

What if the website structure changes and my data feed breaks?

We proactively monitor your data pipelines. If a site change causes a break, our system alerts our engineering team immediately. We have a rapid response process to adapt our scrapers, minimizing downtime. For mission-critical data, we offer service level agreements (SLAs) that guarantee a swift resolution.

Can you set up ongoing, real-time data collection?

Yes. We provide both one-time "snapshots" of data and continuous, scheduled monitoring. We can set up feeds to deliver data at any frequency you need—hourly, daily, weekly, or in near real-time—giving you a live MRI of your market.

Can I request a proof of concept before committing?

Yes, every engagement begins with a scoped data sample so you can validate quality and format before scaling up.

How much does it cost?

At MRI Data, we provide custom quotes because every data insight need is unique, just like every MRI scan is tailored to the patient. A one-time extraction of a simple website will cost significantly less than a continuous, real-time data feed from a complex, global source.
Our pricing is transparent and is based on three core factors:
- Complexity: how difficult is it to access and structure the data? (e.g., a simple blog vs. a JavaScript-heavy web app)
- Volume: How much data do you need? (e.g., 100 product pages vs. 10 million)
- Frequency: Do you need a one-time snapshot or an ongoing monitoring service?

It's simple. Contact us for a free consultation. We'll discuss your data needs, the target sources, and the insights you're hoping to find. From there, we'll provide a detailed project plan and quote. Our process is designed to be a collaborative diagnosis from day one. We are one message away!
